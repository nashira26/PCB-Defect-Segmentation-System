# PCB-Defect-Segmentation-System

Printed Circuit Boards (PCB) are an essential component of almost all electronic products, and their quality directly impacts the reliability and performance of electronic products. Traditional quality inspection methods are manual and subject to human errors, resulting to lower accuracy and productivity. 
This project successfully developed and evaluated a UNet based model for multi-class PCB defect segmentation, achieving pixel-level localization for six distinct fault types. The primary methodology was the implementation of a Combined Focal and Dice Loss function, which directly addressed the severe class imbalance inherent in PCB inspection data. The model achieved a Mean IoU of 0.8571 on the validation set. This score establishes a strong baseline, confirming the model’s robustness against the background dominated input This pixel-level approach significantly surpasses the localization capabilities of traditional object detection methods, offering higher precision for industrial quality control systems. Future improvements can be made by adopting advanced UNet architecture and better data preprocessing.
